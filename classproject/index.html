About Me

My name is Alec O'Connor and I am originally from Hudson, New Hampshire. I attended college at The Pennsylvania State University (Penn State) where I earned a Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering. Throughout my time at Penn State, I participated in the Air Force Reserve Officer Training Corps (ROTC) Program where I earned a commission as an active duty U.S. Air Force Officer. Following graduation, I received my first Air Force assignment and was lucky enough to get stationed close to home at Hanscom Air Force Base in Bedford, Massachusetts. I have worked at Hanscom for three years as a developmental engineer working on both airfield weather and air traffic systems.

During the early years of my career, I worked with a lot of computer hardware and software. I quickly realized that I wanted to work with computer hardware and software, so I applied to the Boston University (BU) Master of Science in Software Development (MSSD) Program and was accepted! I am about halfway through the MSSD Program; I have completed courses focused on Python coding, Java coding, software design patterns, software algorithms, and information systems. When I am not working or studying, I like to indulge in my favorite hobbies which include cooking, watching movies, and playing computer games.

Cast Iron Chicken and Chorizo Paella

Ingredients Quantity
Saffron threads 1/2 tsp
Chicken broth 3 1/4 cups
Olive oil 1 tbsp
Dry-cured Spanish Chorizo, 1/4 inch slices 6 oz
Boneless chicken breast, 1 1/2 inch pieces 1 lb
Black pepper 1/2 tsp
Sea salt 1 1/2 tsp
Onion, diced 3/4 cup
Red bell pepper, diced 1 small pepper
Garlic, minced 1 tbsp
Paprika 1/2 tsp
Canned diced tomatoes, undrained 14.5 oz
Arborio rice 1 1/2 cups
Frozen peas 1 cup
Fresh parsley, chopped 2 tbsp
Lemon wedges, sliced 1 lemon

Directions

  1. Stir together saffron and 1/4 cup of the chicken broth in a small bowl. Let stand at room temperature for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat olive oil in a 12-inch cast iron skillet over medium-high heat. Add chorizo and cook until browned. Remove chorizo and transfer to a plate.
  3. Add chicken, black pepper, and 1 tsp of sea salt to the skillet. Cook chicken over medium-high heat until lightly browned. Remove chicken and transfer to the plate with chorizo.
  4. Add onion and bell pepper to the skillet and cook over medium-high heat until tender.
  5. Stir in garlic and paprika and cook until fragrant. Stir in diced tomatoes and rice, and cook until the liquid is almost fully absorbed.
  6. Stir in the saffron mixture and remaining 3 cups of chicken broth and 1/2 tsp of sea salt. Bring to a simmer over medium-high heat.
  7. Return chorizo and chicken to the skillet and stir lightly to cover partially with rice. Cook, uncovered and undisturbed, over low-medium heat for about 20 minutes.
  8. When the chicken and rice is tender and the liquid is almost fully absorbed, remove the skillet from heat.
  9. Cover and let stand at room temperature until the liquid is fully absorbed. Garnish with parsley and lemon wedges. Serve immediately.
